Article I. Declaration.

I, TESTATOR, of Richmond, Virginia, being of sound and disposing mind, memory and understanding, do make, publish and declare this to be my Last Will and Testament, hereby revoking any and all Wills and Codicils at any time heretofore made by me.

Article II. Payment of Debts and Charges

My Executor shall pay or arrange for the payment of the following obligations:

  1. All of my legally enforceable debts, my charitable pledges, and all taxes upon my estate without apportionment of estate, inheritance or other death taxes.
  2. The expenses of my funeral, including the costs of a suitable burial lot, interment, burial marker or monument and perpetual care thereof or such of these as in the sole discretion of my Executor are deemed appropriate.
  3. The expenses of delivering my tangible personal property, including transportation, storage and insurance, as a cost of administering my estate.

Article III. Tangible Personal Property.

I may leave a list bearing my signature which disposes of certain items of tangible personal property, and I direct that any such list be given effect in accordance with the provisions of Section 64.2-400 of the Code of Virginia.

Article V. Executor and Powers

  1. I appoint EXECUTOR NAME as my Executor. In the event my Executor has predeceased me, or is otherwise unable to serve, I appoint EXECUTOR ALTERNATE to be my substitute Executor. I specifically direct that no bond or other security be required of my Executor while acting in such capacity.
  2. In addition to the powers granted by law, I grant my Executor all of the powers set forth in Section 64.2-105 of the Code of Virginia.
  3. Any person who fails to survive me by 30 days shall be deemed to have predeceased me for the purposes of this will.
  4. If any tangible personal property described in this will vests in a minor, I authorize my Executor to deliver such property to the minor at such time or times as my Executor may deem advisable, notwithstanding the fact that such person is a minor; and my Executor may do so without liability whatsoever for making such delivery. My Executor is also authorized, in the case of a minor, to sell any or all tangible personal property which my Executor considers not to be suitable for distribution to a minor, and to distribute the proceeds as part of my Residuary Estate.
  5. The Executor shall be authorized to distribute a minor’s share of my estate to a Custodian selected by the Executor under Section 64.2-1900 et seq of the Code of Virginia, the Virginia Uniform Transfers to Minors Act and, if the Executor deems it appropriate, to designate age 21 as the date of termination of such custodianship.
